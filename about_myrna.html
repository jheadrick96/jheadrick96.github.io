Myrna Rosen

How it Began



Myrna’s first pen was given to her while she was on bedrest with her first child. Her mother put a pen

and a passage about Arnold Bank into her hands to copy. Although Myrna wasn’t confident that she could pursue this interest, she didn't know that his was the beginning of her whole career.

The Beginning

Building Confidence



Myrna started with envelopes. She

wrote envelopes, thank you cards,

and party invitations for friends. Her elegant hand quickly spread with increasing demand.

Signing Up



It was nearly five years before Myrna worked up the confidence

to sign up for a calligraphy class with Arnold Bank at Carnegie

Mellon on Saturday. The well-

known calligraphy master of the class, Arnold Bank, was notorious for his harsh critique and strict instruction.

I wrote 900,000 envelopes- that’s an exaggeration, but I wrote and wrote and wrote and wrote!

Meeting the Master



It only was only 3 weeks before Arnold Bank took a liking to Myrna. Then, only a matter of time before Myrna Rosen became his apprentice.


Every calligraphy student of Carnegie Mellon will recognize

their first assignment in the Calligraphy I: to write out, in your own hand, the Gettysburg Address. Myrna Rosen herself had such a task assignment by Arnold Bank in her first calligraphy class

Living Two Lives




While Myrna steadily grew as a calligraphy master, both nationally and internationally, Myrna Rosen was and is both wife and mother. She often recalls her late nights working in her basement studio to practice her hands, and work on freelance jobs.

A New-Found Passion


When Myrna began to learn calligraphy she says "she never dreamed" that she would become a teacher. Now she can barely imagine a life without the opportunity to share this passion with others. Myrna herself claims that she continues learning each day with
