Myrna Rosen

Myrna’s first pen was given to her while she was on bedrest with her first child. Her mom put a pen and a passage about Arnold Bank into her hands to copy. Although Myrna wasn’t confident that she could pursue this interest, she tried.

The Beginning

Myrna started with envelopes. She wrote envelopes, thank you cards, and party invitations for friends- and her elegant hand quickly spread with increasing demand.

It was nearly five years before Myrna worked up the confidence to apply to take a calligraphy class at Carnegie Mellon on the weekends. The well-known calligraphy master of the class, Arnold Bank, was notorious for his harsh critique and strict instruction. Yet, it only was only 3 weeks before he took a liking to Myrna. Then it was only a matter of time  before Myrna Rosen became his apprentice.

I wrote 900,000 envelopes- that’s an exaggeration, but I wrote and wrote and

wrote and wrote!

Every calligraphy student of Carnegie Mellon will recognize their first assignment in the Calligraphy I: to write out, in your own hand, the Gettysburg Address. Myrna Rosen herself had such a task assignment by Arnold Bank in her first calligraphy class

As Myrna steadily grew as a well known calligraphy master, both nationally and internationally, Myrna Rosen was and is both wife and mother. She often recalls her late nights working in her basement studio to practice her hands.
